Our Hybrid Approach: Rules + ML

Why Hybrid Architecture?

We designed a hybrid system combining rules-based lexicon matching with ML models:

  • Rules (Primary): Perfect precision (1.000), zero false positives, culturally validated
  • ML (Fallback): Contextual patterns, handles complex cases rules miss
  • Hybrid Router: 70% rules + 30% ML weighting balances speed and coverage
  • Explainable: Every correction traceable to rule or ML decision

Pattern Matching Strategy

Each of the 19 core concepts (chairman, policeman, nurse, etc.) includes detection pattern variants:

  • Capitalization variants: chairman, Chairman, CHAIRMAN
  • Article variants: chairman, the chairman, a chairman
  • Context patterns: "chairman will", "chairman's report"

This ensures detection regardless of how terms appear in text while maintaining perfect precision.

Why Different Numbers Matter

Different languages have different numbers of gendered terms. English has extensive occupation-based gendering (chairman, policeman, waitress). Swahili marks gender through context, not morphology. French has grammatical gender but fewer biased occupation terms. Gikuyu has noun classes requiring different detection strategies. We don't artificially inflate numbers - we adapt to linguistic reality.
